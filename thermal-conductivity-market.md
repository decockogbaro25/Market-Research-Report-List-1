<p><ul><li>Heat Wire Method</li><li>Light Flash Method</li><li>Heat Flow Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The thermal conductivity market comprises various methods for measuring thermal conductivity, including the Heat Wire Method, Light Flash Method, and Heat Flow Method. The Heat Wire Method involves embedding a wire in the material and measuring temperature changes over time. The Light Flash Method utilizes a short laser pulse to assess thermal response, allowing for rapid measurements. The Heat Flow Method, commonly used for insulating materials, calculates thermal conductivity by analyzing heat transfer through a specimen. Each method serves distinct applications based on material properties.</p></p>
