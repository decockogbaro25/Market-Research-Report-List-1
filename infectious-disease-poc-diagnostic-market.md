<p><p>The infectious disease point-of-care (POC) diagnostic market comprises several key types. Lateral flow immunoassay offers quick results via simple test strips, while agglutination tests utilize particle clumping for pathogen detection. Flow-through tests involve liquid samples flowing through a membrane for analysis. Molecular diagnostics employ techniques such as PCR to identify pathogens at a genetic level, providing high sensitivity and specificity. Additionally, other emerging technologies include biosensors and microfluidics, expanding options for rapid and accurate disease diagnosis.</p></p>
