<p><strong>The Handheld Fundus Camera (Retinal Camera) Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Mydriasis</li><li>Non-mydriatic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The handheld fundus camera market is divided into mydriatic and non-mydriatic segments. Mydriatic cameras require pupil dilation to capture detailed retinal images, making them suitable for comprehensive examinations but necessitating extra preparation time for patients. In contrast, non-mydriatic cameras can obtain retinal images without dilation, offering a quicker and more comfortable experience, especially for screening in primary care settings. Both types aim to enhance diagnostic capabilities in ophthalmology while catering to different clinical needs.</p></p>
