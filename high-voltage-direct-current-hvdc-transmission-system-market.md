<p><p>The High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) transmission system market is primarily categorized into three types: Line Commutated Converter (LCC), Voltage Source Converter (VSC), and others. LCC technology utilizes thyristors for stable, large-scale power transmission, often over long distances. VSC technology, using modern power electronics, allows for enhanced control, grid interconnection, and integration of renewable energy sources. Other systems may include various emerging technologies or hybrid configurations, focusing on efficiency, reliability, and lower environmental impact in power transmission networks.</p></p>
